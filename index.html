Advances in energy systems, advanced materials, manufacturing, and climate science increasingly depend on the ability to model and optimise complex physical processes across multiple spatial and temporal scales. These systems are typically governed by coupled, nonlinear dynamics described by partial differential equations and multi-physics models, whose numerical simulation can be computationally expensive and difficult to scale.

Machine learning is transforming scientific computing by allowing data-driven and physics-informed approaches for modelling, simulation, and optimisation. With recent developments (physics-informed learning, neural operators, differentiable solvers, and surrogate modelling) opening new possibilities for accelerating high-fidelity simulations, improving predictive accuracy, and enabling real-time decision-making in complex physical systems.

This workshop brings together researchers from machine learning, applied mathematics, physics, and engineering that focus on scientific machine learning, and applications of these methods for energy and material science.

We invite submissions from academia, industry, and national laboratories working on machine learning for complex physical systems.

Format: Half-day workshop with paper presentations and a keynote talk.
Audience: Researchers, engineers, and data scientists working on scientific ML, hybrid modelling, and multi-scale simulation in energy, materials, climate, and manufacturing.
